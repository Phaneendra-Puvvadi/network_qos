📚 How Our System Works - Step by Step

Step 1: Packet Capture & Classification

When packets arrive at the router, our system examines each packet's header information including source/destination ports, protocol type (TCP/UDP), and packet size. Based on these characteristics, packets are automatically classified into three categories:

  • Real-time (High Priority): VoIP calls, video conferencing, online gaming
  • Interactive (Medium Priority): Web browsing, email, SSH sessions
  • Bulk (Low Priority): File downloads, software updates, backups

Step 2: Congestion Prediction

Our router continuously monitors network conditions by analyzing three key metrics:

  • Link Utilization: Percentage of bandwidth being used
  • Queue Occupancy: How full the packet buffers are
  • Packet Arrival Rate: How quickly packets are arriving

These metrics are combined into a congestion score (0-1). When the score exceeds 0.6, congestion is predicted BEFORE it actually happens.

Step 3: Adaptive Priority Adjustment

Normal Conditions: All packet types are forwarded at their assigned priority levels (5:3:2 ratio for High:Medium:Low).

During Predicted Congestion: The system automatically adapts:

  • High-priority traffic (video calls) gets protected - forwarded immediately
  • Medium-priority traffic (web browsing) experiences slight delays
  • Low-priority traffic (downloads) is significantly throttled

Step 4: Weighted Fair Queuing (WFQ)

Packets are placed into separate queues based on priority. The router uses Weighted Fair Queuing to serve packets:

  • For every scheduling cycle: Forward 5 high-priority packets, 3 medium-priority packets, 2 low-priority packets
  • During congestion: This ratio adapts to protect time-sensitive traffic
  • After congestion clears: Normal ratios are gradually restored

Step 5: DSCP Marking & Forwarding

Before forwarding, each packet's IP header is marked with a DSCP (Differentiated Services Code Point) value:

  • High Priority: DSCP 46 (Expedited Forwarding)
  • Medium Priority: DSCP 34 (Assured Forwarding 41)
  • Low Priority: DSCP 10 (Assured Forwarding 11)

This allows downstream routers to also prioritize these packets correctly.

💡 Key Benefits

Proactive

Predicts congestion 2-3 seconds before it happens, preventing delays rather than reacting to them.

Lightweight

Uses simple mathematical models (EWMA) instead of heavy machine learning, ensuring low latency.

Fair

Prevents starvation of low-priority flows while protecting time-sensitive applications.
